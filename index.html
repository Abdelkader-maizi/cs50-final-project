Introduction

Algeria is a country in North Africa with rich history, diverse culture, and stunning landscapes, from the Sahara Desert to the Mediterranean coastline.

History

Algeria has a rich history influenced by Berbers, Arabs, Ottomans, and French colonial rule, gaining independence in 1962.

Culture

A blend of Arab, Berber, and Mediterranean traditions. Music, festivals, and hospitality define Algerian culture.

Geography

Largest country in Africa: Sahara Desert, Atlas Mountains, and Mediterranean coastline. Climate ranges from arid to Mediterranean.

Cuisine

Flavorful dishes like couscous, tajine, and chorba. Spices such as cumin, coriander, and cinnamon are widely used.

Tourism

Tourist attractions include Roman ruins, the Casbah of Algiers, Sahara adventures, and Mediterranean beaches.
